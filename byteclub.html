What you'll actually understand after playing
  • Attacker Mindset

    Attackers don't strike randomly — they follow a sequence. The Cyber Kill Chain maps every stage from the first bit of reconnaissance to the final objective. Playing the attack side teaches you how adversaries think, plan, and move, and why disrupting any single stage can collapse the entire operation.

  • Defender Mindset

    Defense isn't one big wall — it's a complete posture. The NIST Cybersecurity Framework gives defenders a structured approach: Identify what you're protecting, Protect it, Detect threats early, Respond fast, and Recover fully — with Govern at the center, providing the policies, roles, and decisions that hold it all together. Every gap in that posture is a stage of the Kill Chain a defender failed to cover.

  • The Cycle of Cyberwarfare

    Attack and defense are never finished — they feed each other. Defenders add controls to disrupt the Kill Chain; attackers find ways around them; defenders adapt again. That perpetual loop is the Cycle of Cyberwarfare — and once you see it, you can use it as a mental model to make sense of any threat, breach, or security decision you'll ever encounter.

  • You are CyberFluent

    Understanding that cycle means you can break down any cybersecurity incident or topic and ask the right questions. It won't make you a cyber expert — but it means you can appropriately challenge one. Instead of waiting for the latest checklist from experts to tell you what to think, you'll have the mental model to think critically yourself. Rather than just accepting what you're told or begrudgingly going along with it, you'll understand enough to be genuinely on the same page and push toward the best solution.

How to Play Byte Club

🎯 Goal

Win by one of two conditions:

1. Hold the 🚩 Data Flag when ⏰ Times Up is drawn from the deck.

2. Play one of every attack type (Kill Chain) AND every defend type (NIST) in front of you, including the earned Govern and Action Objectives cards.

🔄 Turn Flow

1. Play cards — double-click cards from your hand to play them. You can play multiple cards per turn.

2. End turn — click End Turn when done.

3. Draw — you automatically draw 1 card.

4. Discard — if you have more than 6 cards, click one to discard.

⚔️ Kill Chain (Attack Cards)

🔍 Recon — peek at a hand or swap played attack cards

⚒️ Weaponize — cancel an opponent's defend card (played out of turn)

📨 Delivery — swap up to 2 played cards with opponents

💥 Exploit — blindly steal 1 card from a target

⚙️ Install — block a target from playing defend cards

📡 C2 — steal the Data Flag, or target gives you a card

🛡️ NIST Framework (Defend Cards)

🔎 Identify — reveal who has the Data Flag or swap played defend cards

🛡️ Protect — block attacks until your next turn

👁️ Detect — view and reorder the top 5 deck cards

🚨 Respond — cancel an attack targeting you (played out of turn)

🔄 Recover — draw until you have 5 cards in hand

⭐ Special Earned Cards

🏛️ Govern — earned by playing all 5 NIST types. Look at all hands, take 1 card from each player, then give 1 back to each.

🎯 Action Obj. — earned by playing all 6 Kill Chain types. The Data Flag holder must give it to you. Cannot be cancelled.

💡 Tips

• Click the ⓘ icons throughout the game for quick help on any element.

• Dotted outlines in your played area show which cards you still need.

• Watch the game log for opponent actions.
